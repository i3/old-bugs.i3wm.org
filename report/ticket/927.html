back_and_forth semantics: "last unfocused workspace"

Description

i'd like to suggest a subtle change in the semantics of back_and_forth, possibily configurable:

currently, back_and_forth stores the workspace that was focused before the last workspace change happened. i'd suggest to define it to store the workspace that last lost focus.

in a single-output configuration, this is equivalent. in a dualhead situation, let's assume i have workspace 2 left, workspace 3 right, and workspace 9 is assigned to the right output too. when i have the left output focused and go to 9, focus jumps to the right monitor, and going back_and_forth keeps 9 at the right monitor and brings me to 2 on the left one.

i'd prefer back_and_forth to take me back to 3 on the right output.

rationale: back_and_forth is like the undo of workspace switching operations. focusing the workspace that last lost focus would reflect that aspect better.

if there are people who prefer the old semantic, it should be configurable, either by a global setting that sets the semantics of back_and_forth, or by establishing a parallel mechanism (let's call it back_and_forth2 for the moment), and users who prefer my behavior just bind their back_and_forth keys to back_and_forth2.

given how exotic this might sound, i don't expect to see a patch on this -- this bug report is more of a reminder to myself to do this, possibly together with some refactoring of the back_and_forth code unless that happened since i last had a look at it.
