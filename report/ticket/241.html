comment:14 Changed 5 years ago by michael

There are different tools like â€‹http://tools.suckless.org/swarp which can be used to warp the pointer to a different display (just call DISPLAY=:0.1 swarp or something like that).

You could also combine this with some additional bindings. For example, say you want the first row of your keyboard to act as additional workspaces on the other screen. In your i3 configuration (for both instances), bind the q, w, e, â€¦ keys. In the instance which runs *not* on the other screen, make them do DISPLAY=:0.1 swarp && DISPLAY=:0.1 i3-msg '1', on the other one just bind them to '1', etc. Get the idea?

For the uninstall: nope, there is no uninstall target. You can just re-install the i3 from arch, it will replace all files (also, there are no differences in the file list, you wonâ€™t end up with unused files on your system).
